# Five-Year Professional Vision (Strategic)

## Where I Want to Be

In 5 years, I want to be a valuable member of my team - the person people go to for answers and advice on projects, the one people aren't scared to talk to. I want to be building things that provide real impact and value to the company and my team.

## What Success Looks Like

**Being the go-to person**: Not just technically competent, but someone who makes the team stronger. When there's a difficult problem, people come to me because they know I'll help them figure it out without judgment.

**Leadership and team value**: I want to be looking after the people around me. Being valuable to my team is how I look after them - delivering excellent work, mentoring others, making the team more effective.

**Building things that matter**: Projects with real impact, not just features that ship and die. Work that provides genuine value to users and the business.

**Technical growth in valuable directions**: Growing expertise in areas that make me more valuable to my team and projects. This might be deeper AI/ML integration in web applications, architectural leadership, or specialized technical domains.

## Growth Path (Flexible Based on Opportunity)

**Current strengths**: Full-stack web development, AI/ML integration (contextual bandits, LLMs, RAG systems), leadership capability, cross-team communication.

**Where I can grow**: 
- **Leadership responsibilities**: Taking charge of projects and teams. This is a complex skill I'll invest time developing because leading well matters in any technical domain.
- **AI integration in web applications**: Bringing LLM capabilities, RAG systems, and ML to web products. This is where web development and AI intersect.
- **Senior technical roles**: Architectural decisions, mentoring developers, technical strategy.
- **Cross-functional collaboration**: I excel at bridging technical and non-technical teams. This skill deepens with experience.

**What I need from a role**: Opportunities to develop skills that matter to me - leadership, AI integration, team building, technical depth. As long as I'm growing in meaningful ways, I'm committed.

## Technical Evolution

**Fundamental skills are transferable**: Problem-solving, system design, testing patterns, time management, understanding structure - these transfer across any technical domain. Even if my focus areas shift over time, the core skills remain valuable.

**AI-powered web applications**: The intersection of web development and AI is expanding rapidly. LLMs, embeddings, RAG, recommendation systems - these aren't separate from web dev, they're the future of it.

**Leadership transcends technology**: Whether I'm leading web developers, AI engineers, or robotics teams, the skills are the same: bringing out the best in people, creating psychological safety, clear communication, strategic thinking.

## Commitment Philosophy

I'm committed as long as:
- I'm developing skills that matter (leadership, technical depth, AI integration)
- I'm providing real value to my team and company
- I'm growing as an engineer and leader
- The work aligns with professional development goals

**Not about timelines**: I don't think "I'll be here exactly 3 years then leave." I think "Am I growing and providing value?" If yes, I stay. If no, we should both reassess.

I want to be valuable, I want to lead, I want to build things that matter. The specific technology stack matters less than the opportunity to grow those capabilities while delivering real impact.

In 5 years: a respected team member, a leader people trust, someone who's built things that provided genuine value. That's success regardless of whether it's in web development, AI engineering, or any other technical domain.
